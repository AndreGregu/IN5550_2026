3: Evaluation 3. draft:
	LLM (GPT) generated summary: 
		The transition from the second to the third draft focused on modeling user-specific behavior and handling the heterogeneity of the data. 
		This was achieved by introducing user-level baseline features (average valence and arousal per user) and training separate models for essays and feeling-word entries. 
		These changes allowed the model to better account for individual differences and the distinct characteristics of the two text types. 
		As a result, arousal prediction improved significantly, particularly in the between-person correlation, and showed modest gains in within-person correlation. 
		However, valence performance decreased slightly, likely due to reduced training data per model and the introduction of additional complexity that was not necessary for capturing lexical sentiment. 
		Overall, these changes demonstrate that modeling user-specific patterns is crucial for capturing emotional dynamics, especially for more challenging targets like arousal, while simpler representations remain sufficient for valence.

	Improvements: 
		Added user-level baseline features: 
			- user_valence_mean
			- user_arousal_mean
		Split the dataset by text type (is_words) and trained seperate models for each
		Introduced intensity-based features: 
			- exclamation_count
			- question_count
			- uppercase_count
			- uppercase_ratio
